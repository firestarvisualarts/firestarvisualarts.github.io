Beautiful Lie

By Cheyenne Clardy

People only love me when
I'm not at peace,
because the me on the outside is a beautiful lie.
This facade keeps the pieces patched together.
At least until I can go home and sew it,
And make it whole again.
This wear and tear has become
Harder to mend when your
Need to keep it together is
a means of short term survival.

A smile all day keeps my tears at bay.
The ability to lie is stronger than my
Will to let go of this pride
And cry.
But more than anything,
Confess my desire to die.

People love me more when I am not
The problem - a burden.
Preaching reassurance
When I continue to fail at practicing it.
"Why am I trapped in a beautiful lie?"
Perhaps I fear that the ugly truth
Will reap the rest of my youth.
Circle of "friends" fading to black
Becoming the mist and fog in my dreams.
Along with that thin thread holding
My sanity -
Finally breaking...

To think giving everyone this beautiful lie is so normal.
Feeding everyone a fallacy
Just to prevent me from losing
The remaining bits of my stability.
