Darkened Waters

By Cheyenne Clardy

Where is the path to shore?
When I am engulfed by these waters.
My feet cannot touch the ground
Yet all I can do is remain calm
Or sink...slowly into the depths.

Holding my breath -
The depths of the blackened abyss.
My words are muted, and even my
Thoughts that I swore had its own
Voice in my head has been silenced.
The demented who plugged my ears,
And gagged me
Have already begun to pursue me.

Dragging me, and pulling me
Down by my throat
There seems to be no escape -
When your sense of certainty
Is deprived by the weights
Of the tangled kelp,
And anchor latched into my ankle
It reaps every choice I could ever fathom.

In these darkened waters,
I can only hold my
Breath and grasp for this sliver
Of hope -
Or scream, desperately for freedom
And drown in the merciless silence.
